About the ACM

Who We Are

ACM, the Association for Computing Machinery, was founded in 1947 for the advancement of computing as a science and a profession, and has become the world’s largest computing society. We’re here to bring it to UT Dallas in style.

What We Do

Enjoy coding, technology, networking (both kinds), & tons of fun? Come on in. At ACM, you'll find everything from industry events, to LAN parties, to UTD's top student hackathon, and more.

Events at ACM

ACM Industry Event Series

At ACM Industry Events, we host representatives from industry to give students an inside view of what it's like to work in the software space, and how to get there.

ACM + Codeburners Coding Competitions

Teaming up with Dr. Ivor Page's award-winning Codeburners, ACM + Codeburners is a series of moderated, multi-tier coding competitionswith prizes, food, and more. Have what it takes?

ACM's Hack_UTD

Hailing in Spring 2015 as UT Dallas' first student hackathon, Hack_UTD is our annual 24-hour marathon of hacking, learning, free food & prizes, and more. Learn more at hackutd.co

ACM Social Events

From food bars to Smash Bros. Tournaments to LAN parties, ACM hosts a variety of socials throughout the semester. Brimming with free food and tons of fun, be sure to check them out.
