We're focused on providing a better engineering experience at UT Dallas and beyond. Take a look at some of the things we do here at ACM.

ACM Industry Series

At ACM Industry Series events, we host comapnies from around Dallas and around the country to give students an inside view of what it's like to work in the software space, and how to get there.

ACM Projects

ACM Projects are ACM's semester-long guided projects for those new to software development, teaching collaborative tools, new technologies, and core development principles earlier and complimentary to curriculum.

ACM Labs

ACM Labs funds and supports student projects with the intent of promoting experimentation with cutting-edge technologies.

Learn even more

ACM Education

ACM Education tutors students in upper-level computer science courses and provides education and resources to prepare students for the industry.

Learn even more

ACM HackUTD

HackUTD is the official student hackathon of UT Dallas and North Texas' largest university hackathon, run by ACM in partnership with Major League Hacking. HackUTD 16 brought over 400 students together for 24 hours of ideas, code, and design, and HackUTD 17's going even bigger.

Let's get you set up

The first step is to fill out our onboarding form which has a few simple questions so that we can get to know a few important things about you. We also ask that you send in a headshot to be placed on our site. Next, you'll need to get acquainted with the tools we use. We use a variety of tools and services to help keep ourselves organized and productive. Having you consistently and effectively using these tools is crucial in keeping our entire organization running smoothly. Lastly, you'll need to get to know our policies. We're pretty chill about things but we do have some rules regarding attendance and performance to ensure everyone is performing their duties effectively.

Our Tools

Here's what we use to stay productive.

Slack
Our team messaging platform. We use to Slack to keep everyone updated about everything that's going on within our organization. We'll send you an invite to join the main ACM Slack team and any other Slack teams relevant to your position.
GitHub
GitHub is where we host our software projects and ACM's wiki content, with information regarding best practices and workflows. Send us your GitHub username so that we can invite you to the teams and repositories relevant to your position.

Sign up at GitHub

Google Drive
We use Google Drive to host all of our media and documents.
Facebook
We have multiple Facebook groups for various different teams and we'll invite you to the ones you'll need to be a part of. We also have a Facebook page that's meant to update our members on upcoming events and initiatives.
Twitter
We use Twitter to publicize our events and initiatives.
MailChimp
MailChimp is our marketing email platform used to publicize events and initiatives to those on our mailing list.
Our Policies

Rules and guidelines you should know.

Attendance
We require all ACM officers to be present at all ACM events pertaining to their team.
Moving Roles
We’re pretty flexible when it comes to changing roles and it’s even possible to hold a role on multiple ACM teams. Our only limitation is that you can't hold more than one position on the same team.
Innovation
At ACM, your officer description is the bare minimum. We want you to make your position yours, and innovate and come up with cool ideas, whether it be evolving your position or making another part of ACM better.
Be Nice
This one goes without saying, but a large part of our culture is ensuring everyone treats each other respectfully.

Lastly, if you have any questions about your position or ACM in general, just let us know.
